üö® The Problem

Food waste is one of the most pressing environmental and social issues of our time. While 828 million people go to bed hungry every night, we waste approximately 1/3 of all food produced globally. This waste occurs at every level - from farms to stores to our own kitchens.


In households alone, the average family throws away $1,500 worth of food annually. This waste contributes significantly to greenhouse gas emissions, water waste, and landfill overflow. Most of this waste is preventable with better planning and awareness.

üí° Smart Tips to Reduce Food Waste

üõí Plan Your Shopping

Make a meal plan before shopping and stick to your list. Check what you already have at home to avoid buying duplicates.

ü•ï Store Food Properly

Learn how to store different foods correctly. Keep bananas separate from other fruits, store potatoes in dark places, and use airtight containers.

üç≥ Use Leftovers Creatively

Transform leftovers into new meals. Yesterday's roasted vegetables can become today's soup or pasta sauce.

üìÖ First In, First Out

Use older items first. Organize your fridge and pantry so older items are in front and easily visible.

ü•∂ Freeze Before It's Too Late

Freeze foods that are close to expiry. Many items can be frozen and used later in cooking.

üí° Understand Date Labels

"Best by" doesn't mean "bad after." Use your senses to determine if food is still good to eat.
